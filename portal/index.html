Strategic Business Findings

1. Strategic Focus on Corporate and Government Segments
Evidence: Wholesale, Government, and Corporate segments drive nearly $8.4B each. "Unknown" segments are negligible.
Impact: High dependence on institutional B2B contracts. Retail is a secondary driver for stability.
Recommendation: Launch a B2B Key Account Management (KAM) program with customized credit terms.
2. Market Preference and Inventory Optimization
Evidence: Tea and Cookies lead, but Beverages and Dairy maintain identical shares (~$5.6B each).
Impact: Diversified risk profile due to balanced demand, but complex SKU management.
Recommendation: Implement Agile Inventory and cross-sell bundles (e.g., Tea + Snacks) to increase ticket.
3. Regional Expansion and Logistics Efficiency
Evidence: Stable performance across Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico without regional outliers.
Impact: Localization is successful, but multi-country logistics increase overhead costs.
Recommendation: Centralize a Regional Distribution Hub to consolidate cross-border supply chains.

Actionable Insights

  • Wholesale (Mayorista) is the top segment with ~$8.45B, while Corporate and Government are very close (~$8.43B), showing a balanced revenue mix.
  • The top products are Tea, Cookies, and Yogurt (~$2.83B each), so cross-category bundles across beverages and snacks can significantly lift the average ticket.
  • Sales are evenly distributed across Brazil, Colombia, and Mexico — this supports investment in a regional logistics hub to reduce per-unit shipping costs.
